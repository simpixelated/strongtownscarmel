In our last meeting we had a very informative discussion on zoning, thanks to the expertise of urban planner [Jeffery Tompkins](https://www.jefferytompkins.com/) as well as Carmel city councilor [Shannon Minnaar](https://www.carmel.in.gov/government/city-council/shannon-minnaar), who sits on the Housing Commission. My summary of the Q&A with some quotes from Mr Tompkins:

- "There's going to be a next Carmel" - the model of good schools plus a walkable downtown with mixed-use is easily replicated. People will choose to live in Westfield, Fishers, etc. over Carmel if the housing is cheaper. We can't depend on demand continuing forever.

- While comprehensive plans might prioritize walkability, small business support, and even transit, the actual zoning code doesn't always match; "you get what you zone for" — and that means mostly single family housing in many cities.

- More flexible zoning allows you as a property owner to "grow with your land". Adaptive re-use and multi-generational living are possible when owners can add density to their homes.

- "Resident developers can grow wealth" - zoning that allows homeowners to add an [ADU](https://www.canva.com/design/DAGzijcO8Zg/ah4_aYvZpXUmjI_NHrvvCw/view) or renovate to create a duplex helps residents participate in the development of Carmel and grow local wealth, instead of leaving development only to the rich or outside developers with "Wall Street money".

I enjoyed the back and forth discussion on how zoning can affect wealth and shape our cities.
